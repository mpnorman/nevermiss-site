A Salesforce alternative for people who just sell from Gmail

Salesforce is the most powerful CRM on the planet. It's also built for companies with dedicated admins, sales ops teams, and implementation budgets. If you're a solo operator or a small team selling from Gmail, Salesforce is like renting an aircraft carrier to cross a lake.

You don't need 2,000 features. You need to stop forgetting follow-ups.

Why Salesforce is overkill for small sellers

  • The real cost isn't $25/month. Salesforce Starter quotes $25/user/month, but once you add essential integrations, email sync, and the time to configure it, the true annual cost for a solo user can reach $2,000–3,000+.
  • It demands data entry. Salesforce only works when you feed it. Every email, every call, every deal update needs to be logged — manually or through integrations that cost extra. Solo sellers spend more time updating the CRM than closing.
  • It lives in its own world. You write emails in Gmail. Then you switch to Salesforce to log the activity, update the deal, check the pipeline. Then you switch back to Gmail. The context-switching is relentless.
  • Complexity breeds abandonment. Salesforce has a steep learning curve. Without someone to maintain it, dashboards go stale, automations break, and the CRM becomes a graveyard of outdated data.

Who should stay on Salesforce

Enterprise teams with 50+ reps, complex sales processes, compliance requirements, and dedicated admins. Salesforce is genuinely the best at what it does — at scale, with proper investment. It's the wrong tool if you're a team of 1–10 running everything from your inbox.
