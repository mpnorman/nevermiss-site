Alternative

A Mailtrack alternative that turns opens into action

Mailtrack is the most popular email tracking extension for Gmail. Install it, send an email, see a green checkmark when it's opened. Simple, effective, free (with a signature badge).

But tracking is only half the equation. Knowing someone opened your proposal three times doesn't help if you forget to follow up. Mailtrack tells you what happened — but nothing about what to do next.

Where Mailtrack stops short

  • Tracking without workflow. Mailtrack shows opens. It doesn't build you a follow-up queue, set next actions, or alert you when deals go stale. The "what now?" is entirely on you.
  • The free signature. Mailtrack's free plan adds "Sent with Mailtrack" to every email. For personal use that's fine. For sales, it signals that you're tracking the recipient — which can feel invasive.
  • No deal context. Open data without deal context is noise. Knowing someone opened your email 5 times is only useful if you also know the deal stage, value, and when you last followed up.
  • No follow-up reminders. Mailtrack has no built-in system for scheduling follow-ups or surfacing overdue conversations. You need a separate tool for that.
  • Data without prioritization. When you track every email, you get a stream of open notifications. Without prioritization, it's hard to tell which opens matter.

Who should stay on Mailtrack

If you only need to know whether someone opened your email — and you don't manage deals or follow-ups — Mailtrack is a clean, lightweight solution. It does one thing and does it well.
