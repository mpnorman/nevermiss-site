A Yesware alternative that doesn't need Salesforce

Yesware is a sales productivity tool that adds email tracking, templates, and meeting scheduling to Gmail and Outlook. Its biggest selling point? Deep Salesforce integration — emails automatically sync to Salesforce records.

That's perfect if you're on Salesforce. If you're not, you're paying for a tool whose core value proposition doesn't apply to you.

Where Yesware assumes too much

  • Salesforce-centric design. Yesware's most valuable features — activity sync, CRM sidebar, opportunity tracking — require a Salesforce subscription. Without it, you're getting a tracking tool at a premium price.
  • Enterprise pricing. Plans start at $15/user/month for basic tracking and go to $65/user/month for the Salesforce integration. The sweet spot is the mid-tier at $35/month — still expensive for solo sellers.
  • Templates over workflow. Yesware leans heavily into email templates and multi-touch campaigns. If your selling is 1:1 and conversational, the template library isn't as useful.
  • No daily follow-up queue. Yesware tracks opens and template usage but doesn't build a prioritized daily list of who to follow up with. You still manage your own follow-up discipline.
  • No stale deal detection. Yesware reports on email activity but doesn't proactively alert you when specific deals have gone quiet.

Who should stay on Yesware

If your team runs Salesforce and needs email activity synced to CRM records, Yesware does that seamlessly. It's also strong for teams that rely on email templates and multi-touch campaigns at scale.

Who NeverMiss is for

People who sell from Gmail and don't use (or want) Salesforce. If your pipeline is in your inbox and your biggest problem is follow-up discipline — not CRM sync — NeverMiss gives you the workflow without the enterprise dependency.
