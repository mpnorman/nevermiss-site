Alternative

A Copper CRM alternative that's lighter and faster

Copper (formerly ProsperWorks) bills itself as "the CRM for Google Workspace." It integrates deeply with Gmail, Google Calendar, and Google Drive — pulling contacts, logging emails, and syncing activities automatically.

That's genuinely useful for teams. But if you're a solo seller or small team, Copper's full CRM machinery — contacts databases, company records, activity logging, custom reports — is more infrastructure than you need. And at $25–119/user/month, you're paying for it whether you use it or not.

Where Copper is too heavy

  • It's still a CRM. Copper has a separate web app with contacts, companies, pipelines, tasks, and reports. The Gmail sidebar is a window into it — not a replacement for it. You still need to maintain the CRM.
  • Price for solo users. The Basic plan starts at $25/user/month. The Professional plan ($59/user) is required for workflow automation. That's $300–700/year for features designed for teams.
  • Auto-logging noise. Copper automatically logs every email interaction into contact records. For team CRMs this is valuable. For a solo seller it creates a firehose of activity data you don't need.
  • No stale deal detection. Copper shows pipeline views but doesn't proactively alert you when individual deals go quiet. You have to notice the silence yourself.
  • No email open tracking. Copper doesn't track email opens. You need a separate extension for that, which means managing two tools instead of one.

If your team lives in Google Workspace and needs a shared CRM with contact records, company data, activity logs, and reporting — Copper's deep Google integration is hard to beat. It's a real CRM that feels native to Google's ecosystem.
