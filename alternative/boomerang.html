A Boomerang alternative that goes beyond snooze

Boomerang was one of the first great Gmail extensions. Snooze an email, schedule a send, get a reminder if nobody replies. Simple and useful.

But if you're selling — managing deals, tracking who opened your proposals, trying to keep 20 conversations from going cold — Boomerang starts to feel like a timer without a system. You get reminded, but you don't get a workflow.

Where Boomerang hits its ceiling

  • Credit-based limits. The free plan gives you a handful of message credits per month. Once you're tracking more than a few threads, you need a paid plan ($5–50/month).
  • No pipeline or deal tracking. Boomerang can remind you to follow up, but it has no concept of deals, stages, or values. You're managing reminders, not a sales process.
  • No stale deal detection. If you forget to set a reminder on one thread, it disappears. Boomerang only tracks what you explicitly tell it to — there's no safety net for the ones that slip through.
  • Reminders without context. When a Boomerang reminder surfaces, you see the email thread — but not whether they opened it, what stage the deal is in, or how long it's been since last contact.
  • AI features replacing core value. Recent versions have leaned into AI writing assistance, which dilutes the product's focus on the follow-up workflow that made it popular.

Who should stay on Boomerang

If you don't sell — if you're using Boomerang for personal email scheduling, meeting reminders, or inbox management — it's still a great tool. It's simple, mature, and does the basics well.
