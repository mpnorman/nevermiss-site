Best email tracking extension for Chrome in 2026

You want to know if they read your email. That's the basic promise of every email tracking extension in the Chrome Web Store. But the extensions differ wildly in what they do with that information — and what else they add to your workflow.

Here's how the main options compare, from pure tracking to full sales tools.

What to look for

  • Tracking accuracy — how reliably does it detect opens? (All use pixel tracking, so accuracy is similar, but presentation matters)
  • Privacy for you — does the free plan brand your emails with "tracked by [tool]"?
  • What happens after the open — does the tool help you act on the data, or just show you a number?
  • Gmail performance — does it slow down your inbox?
  • Price — what do you actually get for free vs. paid?

Our pick

NeverMiss — if you want tracking that feeds a follow-up workflow.

Open data is only useful if it changes what you do next. NeverMiss connects tracking to your daily follow-up queue: threads with high engagement surface higher, and the open count shows up in-line with deal context. Tracking isn't a standalone feature — it's part of the system.

Extension-by-extension breakdown

NeverMiss

A Chrome extension that turns Gmail into a follow-up command center. Open tracking shows who's reading, the Today queue shows who to contact, and stale deal nudges catch what slips through.

  • Strengths: Tracking integrated into a sales workflow, stale detection, affordable
  • Limitations: No link click tracking (yet), newer extension
  • Price: Free forever (with limits) or $12.99/month

Mailtrack

The most popular pure tracking extension. Green checkmarks show whether your email was opened. Clean, simple, and well-established.

  • Strengths: Lightweight, popular, generous free plan
  • Limitations: "Sent with Mailtrack" on free plan, no follow-up or deal features
  • Price: Free (with branding) to $9.99/month

Streak

A full CRM inside Gmail with email tracking included. Open and link tracking are built into the larger CRM workflow.

  • Strengths: Tracking in CRM context, shared pipelines, mature
  • Limitations: Heavy extension, expensive for solo use, CRM overhead for just tracking
  • Price: $15–49/user/month

Boomerang

Email productivity tool with read receipts on paid plans. Best known for snooze and send-later, not tracking.

  • Strengths: Great send-later feature, AI writing, established brand
  • Limitations: Tracking is a secondary feature, credit-based system, no deal context
  • Price: $4.98–49.98/month

Mixmax

Sales engagement platform with real-time open and link tracking. Designed for SDR teams running sequences.

  • Strengths: Real-time notifications, link tracking, sequence integration
  • Limitations: Expensive, complex setup, overkill for individuals
  • Price: $29–89/user/month

Bottom line

If you just want to know "did they open it?" — Mailtrack is the simplest option. If you want tracking as part of a full CRM, Streak or Mixmax deliver. If you want tracking woven into a daily follow-up system that helps you act on the data — NeverMiss bridges the gap.
