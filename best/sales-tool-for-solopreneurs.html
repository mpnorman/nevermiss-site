Best sales tool for solopreneurs in 2026

You're a one-person business. You do the selling, the delivery, the invoicing, and the follow-ups. You don't have a sales team, a sales ops person, or time to maintain a CRM. But you still need to close deals — and that means you need a system.

The right sales tool for a solopreneur isn't Salesforce scaled down. It's a tool built for how solo sellers actually work: from the inbox, in the margins between client work, with zero admin overhead.

What solopreneurs actually need

  • Follow-up discipline — never forget to check in on a proposal or lead
  • Deal awareness — know what's in play, what stage it's in, and what needs attention today
  • Open signals — understand who's engaged before deciding where to spend your limited time
  • Stale detection — get alerted when deals go quiet, before it's too late to recover
  • Zero maintenance — no data entry, no contact management, no weekly CRM cleanup sessions

Tool-by-tool breakdown

NeverMiss

A Chrome extension that adds follow-up queues, deal tracking, open data, and stale alerts to Gmail. Built specifically for the one-person sales workflow.

  • Strengths: Zero maintenance, follow-up discipline built-in, affordable, lightweight
  • Limitations: Gmail-only, not a full CRM, no mail merge or sequences
  • Price: Free forever (with limits) or $12.99/month

Google Sheets

The DIY approach. Create a spreadsheet with deal name, stage, value, and next follow-up date. Sort by date each morning.

  • Strengths: Free, infinitely customizable, no learning curve
  • Limitations: Entirely manual, no connection to email, goes stale within weeks
  • Price: Free

Streak

A full CRM that lives inside Gmail. Pipelines, contacts, mail merge, and tracking — built for teams but usable solo.

  • Strengths: Feature-rich, team-ready, mature product
  • Limitations: Slows Gmail, expensive solo ($49/mo Pro), CRM management overhead
  • Price: $15–49/user/month

HubSpot Free CRM

A full CRM with a Gmail extension. The free tier is generous but the product pushes you toward paid features.

  • Strengths: Free tier, strong reporting at scale, well-known brand
  • Limitations: CRM maintenance required, upsell-heavy, overkill for solopreneurs
  • Price: Free (limited) to $90/user/month

Notion CRM

Build a custom CRM using Notion databases, boards, and relations. Flexible and visual.

  • Strengths: Highly customizable, good for visual thinkers, affordable
  • Limitations: Entirely manual, no email integration, requires setup and ongoing maintenance
  • Price: Free to $10/month

Bottom line
