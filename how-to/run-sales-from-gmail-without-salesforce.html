How to run sales from Gmail without Salesforce

The conventional wisdom says you need a CRM to sell. Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive — pick your platform, import your contacts, configure your pipelines, and start logging activities. For enterprise sales teams, that's the right move.

But if you're a solo operator or small team, and 90% of your selling happens in email? You need a sales workflow, not a CRM. And that workflow can run entirely from Gmail.

What a Gmail-based sales workflow looks like

Strip a sales process to its essentials and you need five things happening every day:

  1. Know what to do today. A prioritized list of who to contact — follow-ups, check-ins, and new outreach.
  2. Send with context. When you open a thread, instantly see the deal stage, value, and last interaction — not just the email text.
  3. Track engagement. Know who opened your emails, how many times, and when. Use that to prioritize and time your replies.
  4. Catch what's slipping. If a deal goes quiet for 5+ days, get alerted before it's too late to recover.
  5. See the big picture. How many deals are active? What's the total pipeline value? Which stage has the most stuck deals?

Salesforce does all of this — plus 500 features you'll never use. The question is whether you can get these five essentials without the overhead.

The Gmail sales stack

  • Gmail — your email client (you're already using this)
  • Google Calendar — for scheduling meetings and calls
  • A follow-up + pipeline extension — the missing layer that turns Gmail into a sales tool

That's it. No Salesforce. No implementation project. No $25/month/user that turns into $3,000/year after add-ons.
