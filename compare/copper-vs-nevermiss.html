Copper vs NeverMiss: CRM vs follow-up tool

Copper is "the CRM for Google Workspace." NeverMiss is a follow-up system for Gmail. One is a full platform with contacts, companies, and reporting. The other adds a sales layer to your inbox without the platform overhead.

The real question isn't which is "better" — it's whether you need a CRM at all.

Where Copper wins

  • Team CRM. Shared pipelines, permissions, activity logs — Copper is built for teams that need a single source of truth.
  • Contact management. Full contact and company records with relationship mapping and interaction history.
  • Google ecosystem. Deep integration with Calendar, Drive, and Sheets. If your team lives in Google Workspace, Copper feels native.
  • Reporting. Pipeline reports, activity metrics, and forecasting dashboards for managers.

Where NeverMiss wins

  • Zero maintenance. No CRM to maintain, no contact records to update, no activities to log. The system builds itself from your email behavior.
  • Email open tracking. Copper doesn't track opens. NeverMiss shows who read your email and how many times.
  • Stale deal detection. NeverMiss proactively flags deals that have gone quiet. Copper requires you to check your pipeline manually.
  • Price. $12.99/month vs. $25–119/user/month. For a solo seller, that's the difference between a tool that pays for itself and a significant overhead cost.
  • No separate app. Everything lives inside Gmail. No tab-switching, no syncing, no "I forgot to update the CRM."

Verdict

Use Copper if you have a team that needs shared CRM data, contact management, and reporting across Google Workspace.

Use NeverMiss if you're a solo seller or small team whose pipeline is in Gmail and whose biggest risk is forgetting to follow up — not a lack of CRM data.
