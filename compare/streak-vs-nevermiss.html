Streak vs NeverMiss: which Gmail CRM is better?

Both Streak and NeverMiss live inside Gmail. Both help you track deals and follow-ups without leaving your inbox. But they're fundamentally different tools with different philosophies.

Streak is a full CRM that happens to live in Gmail. NeverMiss is a follow-up command center that intentionally isn't a CRM. Here's an honest comparison.

Where Streak wins

  • Full CRM features. Streak has custom fields, filtered views, mail merge, reporting, and shared pipelines. If you need a traditional CRM but want it in Gmail, Streak is the most complete option.
  • Team collaboration. Shared pipelines, assigned deals, and team-level views make Streak viable for small sales teams who need to coordinate.
  • Maturity. Streak has been around since 2012. It's battle-tested with a large user base and established integrations.

Where NeverMiss wins

  • Follow-up discipline. NeverMiss is built around a daily queue — a prioritized list of who to follow up with today. Streak relies on you checking the pipeline to find what needs attention.
  • Stale deal detection. NeverMiss automatically flags deals that have gone silent. Streak requires you to notice the silence yourself by reviewing pipeline dates.
  • Performance. Streak adds noticeable weight to Gmail. NeverMiss is designed to be lightweight.
  • Simplicity. Five features, no configuration required. Install and start using it in under a minute. Streak requires pipeline setup, field customization, and workflow configuration.
  • Send-time optimization. NeverMiss learns when each contact opens email and suggests send windows. Streak doesn't offer this.
  • Price. NeverMiss Pro is $12.99/month. Streak Pro (for tracking + pipelines) is $49/month.

The real question: CRM or command center?

This comparison isn't really about features. It's about what you need:

Choose Streak if...

You want a full CRM and you're willing to invest time configuring it. You need shared pipelines, mail merge, or detailed reporting. You have a small team that needs to collaborate on deals. You're okay with Gmail being slower in exchange for more features.

Choose NeverMiss if...

You don't want a CRM — you want to stop forgetting follow-ups. You're a solo operator or small team that needs daily follow-up discipline, not a database. You want open tracking, stale deal alerts, and a daily queue without configuring anything. You want Gmail to stay fast.

They're not competing for the same job. Streak is for people who need CRM functionality. NeverMiss is for people who need follow-up discipline. Knowing which one you need is more important than any feature comparison.
