Boomerang vs NeverMiss: which Gmail extension is better?

Both are Chrome extensions for Gmail. Both help you follow up. But they solve different problems. Boomerang is an email productivity tool — snooze, schedule, remind. NeverMiss is a sales follow-up system — queue, track, prioritize.

Here's the full comparison.

The core difference

Boomerang asks: "When should this email come back?" It's a timer. You set it, you get reminded, you decide what to do.

NeverMiss asks: "What should I do today?" It's a system. Your daily queue is pre-built, prioritized by engagement data, and enriched with deal context.

Where Boomerang wins

  • Email scheduling. Boomerang's send-later is polished and reliable. Write now, send at 8am Tuesday.
  • Inbox pause. Block new emails from arriving while you focus. Simple and useful.
  • AI writing. Respondable scores your email's likelihood of getting a reply and suggests improvements.
  • Non-sales use cases. Personal reminders, meeting follow-ups, inbox management — Boomerang handles all of it.

Where NeverMiss wins

  • Sales workflow. A structured daily queue beats scattered reminders when you're managing multiple deals.
  • Deal awareness. Stage, value, and next step on every thread — Boomerang has none of this.
  • Stale detection. NeverMiss catches deals you forgot to track. Boomerang only reminds you about emails you explicitly snoozed.
  • Open tracking integrated into workflow. Not just "did they open it" but "how does that change my priority today?"
  • Pricing clarity. Flat $12.99/month vs. Boomerang's credit system where heavy users hit limits quickly.

Verdict

Use Boomerang if you need email scheduling, inbox management, and general productivity features — especially if you don't sell.

Use NeverMiss if you sell from Gmail and need follow-up discipline, deal tracking, and pipeline visibility — not just reminders.

They can even run side by side: Boomerang for scheduling, NeverMiss for the sales workflow.
