Mailtrack vs NeverMiss: which Gmail extension is better?

Mailtrack is the most popular email tracking extension for Gmail — over 4 million users. NeverMiss is newer and less known. But they're solving different problems, and understanding the difference helps you pick the right one.

The core difference

Mailtrack answers: "Did they read my email?" Full stop. It's a signal tool. Green check: opened. Gray check: not yet.

NeverMiss answers: "They read my email — now what?" It connects the open data to a follow-up workflow. A thread that's been opened 4 times but hasn't gotten a reply surfaces higher in your daily queue, with the deal stage and value visible.

Where Mailtrack wins

  • Simplicity. If you just want to know whether someone opened your email, Mailtrack does that with zero friction. Install, send, check.
  • Generous free plan. Unlimited tracking on the free tier (with email signature branding). Most tracking extensions limit free usage.
  • Link click tracking. Mailtrack tracks when recipients click links in your email — useful for proposals with embedded links.
  • Lower price. If you remove the branding and add features, Mailtrack tops out at $9.99/month.

Where NeverMiss wins

  • Follow-up workflow. Tracking without follow-ups is observation without action. NeverMiss builds a daily queue from your tracked threads.
  • Deal context. Open data means more when you see it alongside the deal stage, value, and days since last contact.
  • Stale detection. Mailtrack tells you about threads you're watching. NeverMiss catches threads you forgot to watch.
  • No email branding. NeverMiss doesn't append signatures or badges to your emails on any plan.
  • Pipeline visibility. See active deals by stage and total value — Mailtrack has no concept of deals.

Verdict

Use Mailtrack if you only need to know who opened your email and don't need follow-up workflow or deal tracking. It's the cheapest, simplest option for pure tracking.

Use NeverMiss if you sell from Gmail and want open tracking as part of a complete follow-up and deal management system. The tracking is table stakes — the workflow is what closes deals.
