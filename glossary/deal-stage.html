Glossary

Deal Stage

A deal stage is the current position of a sales opportunity in your pipeline, indicating how far the deal has progressed from initial contact toward a close.

Deal stage vs. pipeline stage

These terms are often used interchangeably. Pipeline stages define the steps in your process. A deal stage is where a specific deal sits within those steps right now. Think of pipeline stages as the columns on a board, and the deal stage as which column a particular deal card is in.

Why tracking deal stage matters

  • Prioritization: A deal in "Negotiation" needs different attention than one in "Lead." Knowing the stage tells you what to do next.
  • Forecasting: Deals in later stages are more likely to close. Knowing where your deals are helps you predict revenue.
  • Stale detection: A deal that's been in "Proposal Sent" for 2 weeks needs attention. A deal in "Lead" for 2 weeks is normal.
  • Context: When you open an email thread, seeing the deal stage instantly reminds you where things left off.
