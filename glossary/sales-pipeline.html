Glossary

Sales Pipeline

A sales pipeline is a visual representation of every active deal you're working, organized by stage — from first contact through to closed-won or closed-lost.

Why you need a pipeline

Without a pipeline, your deals exist in your head, scattered across email threads and maybe a spreadsheet. A pipeline answers three critical questions at a glance:

  1. What's in play? — How many active deals, and what's the total potential value?
  2. Where is each deal? — Which stage is every opportunity in right now?
  3. What needs attention? — Which deals are stuck, stale, or ready to close?

Common pipeline stages

A simple pipeline for a solo seller or small team:

  • Lead — Initial contact, not yet qualified
  • Contacted — You've reached out, awaiting response
  • Warm / Interested — They've responded positively
  • Proposal Sent — You've sent a quote or proposal
  • Negotiation — Discussing terms, timeline, or scope
  • Closed-Won — Deal signed
  • Closed-Lost — Deal didn't happen

Keep it simple. Too many stages create busywork. Too few hide what's actually happening.
