Glossary

Stale Deal

A stale deal is an open opportunity that has had no activity — no emails, calls, or status updates — for a defined period, signaling it may be at risk of being lost.

Why stale deals are dangerous

Stale deals are the silent killer of sales pipelines. Unlike a clear rejection, a stale deal gives you nothing — no signal, no feedback, just silence. That silence feels temporary ("they're probably just busy"), which is why most sellers wait too long to act.

The reality: the longer a deal stays stale, the harder it is to revive. After 7–10 days of silence on a typical B2B deal, your probability of closing drops significantly. After 2+ weeks, you're often starting over.

What causes deals to go stale

  • You forgot to follow up. The most common cause. You meant to check in on Thursday, got busy, and two weeks passed.
  • The prospect got distracted. They were interested, but something else took priority. Without a nudge from you, your deal slipped off their radar.
  • Your email got buried. It arrived at the wrong time, got lost in a crowded inbox, or went to spam. They never saw it.
  • They're evaluating alternatives. Silence sometimes means they're talking to your competitors. The first seller to re-engage often wins.

How to detect stale deals

The simplest approach: define a threshold (e.g., 5 days of no activity) and check your pipeline against it regularly. The problem is "regularly" — on busy weeks, the audit gets skipped, and those are exactly the weeks deals go stale.
