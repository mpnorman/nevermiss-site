Email Open Tracking

Email open tracking is technology that detects when a recipient opens your email, typically by embedding a tiny invisible image (tracking pixel) that pings a server when loaded.

How it works

When you send an email with tracking enabled, the tool inserts a 1x1 transparent pixel image. When the recipient's email client loads this image, it sends a request to the tracking server — recording that the email was opened, when, and from where.

Most tracking tools also record repeat opens, so you can see if someone opened your email once (glanced at it) or five times (seriously considering it).

Limitations

  • Image blocking: Some email clients block external images by default. If the recipient never loads images, the open won't register.
  • Apple Mail Privacy Protection: Apple's Mail Privacy Protection pre-loads tracking pixels automatically, which can inflate open counts for Apple Mail users.
  • Privacy clients: Tools like Hey.com and Tutanota strip tracking pixels entirely.

Treat open data as a useful signal, not absolute truth. A confirmed open is reliable. The absence of an open doesn't necessarily mean they didn't see it.

How to use open data effectively

  • Prioritize follow-ups: Someone who opened your proposal 4 times is warmer than someone who opened it once. Follow up with the engaged ones first.
  • Time your sends: If a contact consistently opens emails at 8am, send your next follow-up at 7:45am.
  • Detect interest: Multiple opens in a short period often mean the prospect is sharing your email internally — a strong buying signal.
  • Know when to pivot: If they've opened 3 emails without replying, the problem isn't visibility — it's your offer or timing.
