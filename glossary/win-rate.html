Glossary

Win Rate

Win rate is the percentage of deals that close successfully out of all deals that reached a qualified stage in your pipeline, calculated as (closed-won ÷ total closed deals) × 100.

What's a good win rate?

  • Cold outreach: 5–15% is common. You're contacting people who didn't ask to hear from you.
  • Warm leads / referrals: 25–40% is typical. The relationship is already started.
  • Inbound (they came to you): 40–60% is achievable when the prospect has already identified their need.

Win rate varies enormously by industry, deal size, and lead source. The important thing isn't the number — it's the trend. If your win rate is improving, your process is working.

How follow-up discipline improves win rate

Most lost deals aren't lost to competitors — they're lost to silence. The prospect was interested, but nobody followed up. The proposal sat unopened. The conversation drifted. Better follow-up discipline directly increases win rate by:

  • Keeping warm deals warm. Timely follow-ups prevent the cooling period that kills momentum.
  • Catching stale deals early. Re-engaging a deal at 5 days of silence is far more effective than at 30 days.
  • Using open data to prioritize. Following up with people who are actively reading your emails improves conversion.
  • Eliminating "I forgot" losses. A follow-up system means no deal dies because you got busy.
