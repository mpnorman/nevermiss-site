Glossary

Email Open Rate

Email open rate is the percentage of sent emails that recipients actually open, calculated as (emails opened ÷ emails delivered) × 100.

What's a good open rate?

It depends on context. Rough benchmarks for sales emails:

  • Cold outreach: 20–40% is solid. Above 40% is excellent.
  • Warm follow-ups: 40–60% is typical when the recipient knows you.
  • Existing clients: 60%+ is normal for active relationships.

These numbers are less reliable than they used to be due to Apple Mail Privacy Protection and image-blocking email clients, which can either inflate or deflate reported rates.

What affects open rate

  • Subject line: The single biggest factor. Specific, personal subject lines dramatically outperform generic ones.
  • Sender name: Emails from a real person's name get opened more than emails from a company name.
  • Send time: Sending when the recipient is actively checking email increases opens. NeverMiss's Send-Time Signal learns each contact's patterns.
  • Deliverability: If your email lands in spam or Promotions, it won't get opened regardless of how good it is.
  • Relationship: Warm contacts open far more than cold prospects. Follow-up persistence builds familiarity.
