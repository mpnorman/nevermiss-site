CRM Inside Gmail

A CRM inside Gmail is a lightweight customer relationship management tool that operates as a Chrome extension or Gmail add-on, so salespeople can manage deals, contacts, and follow-ups without leaving their inbox.

Why a CRM inside Gmail makes sense

If you sell via email, your conversations are already in Gmail. A separate CRM forces you to duplicate information — sending the email in one app, logging it in another, checking the pipeline in a third. A CRM inside Gmail eliminates the context-switching by putting deal data where the conversations happen.

The spectrum of Gmail CRMs

  • Full CRMs in Gmail: Tools like Streak that put a complete CRM (pipelines, contacts, mail merge, reporting) inside the Gmail interface. Powerful, but add weight and complexity.
  • CRM sidebars: Tools like HubSpot and Copper that add a sidebar panel to Gmail, showing CRM data alongside your email. The CRM still lives elsewhere — the sidebar is a window into it.
  • Follow-up command centers: Tools like NeverMiss that don't try to be a CRM at all — instead adding follow-up queues, deal context, and tracking directly to Gmail. Lighter than a CRM, focused on preventing deals from going stale.

Which approach is right for you

If you need shared pipelines, detailed reporting, and team collaboration — a full Gmail CRM like Streak makes sense. If you already use HubSpot or Salesforce, a sidebar extension connects Gmail to your existing data.
