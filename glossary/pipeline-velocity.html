Pipeline Velocity

Pipeline velocity measures how quickly deals move through your sales pipeline and convert to revenue. It's calculated as: (Number of Deals x Average Deal Value x Win Rate) / Average Sales Cycle Length.

What pipeline velocity tells you

Pipeline velocity is a single number that captures the health of your entire sales operation. A higher velocity means you're generating revenue faster. A declining velocity means something is stuck — deals are taking longer, values are shrinking, or your win rate is dropping.

For solo sellers, you don't need to calculate this precisely. The concept is what matters: are deals moving forward, or are they stalling?

The four levers of pipeline velocity

  • Number of deals. More qualified opportunities in your pipeline increases velocity. Focus on generating leads that actually convert, not just filling the top of the funnel.
  • Average deal value. Higher-value deals increase velocity. Upselling, better qualifying, or targeting larger accounts all help.
  • Win rate. Closing a higher percentage of deals increases velocity. Better follow-up, sharper proposals, and faster response times all improve win rate.
  • Sales cycle length. Shorter cycles increase velocity. Reducing friction, responding faster, and proactive follow-ups compress timelines.

How follow-up discipline affects velocity

Follow-up discipline is the highest-leverage activity for a solo seller because it directly impacts three of the four levers:

  • Win rate increases when deals don't die from silence. Most lost deals aren't rejected — they're forgotten.
  • Cycle length decreases when you follow up on time instead of 5 days late. Every delayed follow-up adds days to your cycle.
  • Deal count increases when you re-engage past clients and nurture leads instead of letting them go cold.
