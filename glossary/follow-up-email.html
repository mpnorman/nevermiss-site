Follow-Up Email

A follow-up email is a subsequent message sent after initial outreach that hasn't yet received a reply, designed to re-engage the recipient and keep the conversation moving forward.

Why follow-up emails matter

Research consistently shows that most sales require multiple touchpoints, but the majority of salespeople stop after one email. The deals that close usually involve 3–7 follow-ups. The ones that die? They die after one unanswered message and silence.

Follow-up emails aren't pestering — they're professional persistence. Most prospects are busy, not uninterested. A well-timed follow-up often arrives at exactly the right moment.

When to send a follow-up

  • After a cold email: 2–3 days later if no reply
  • After a proposal: 3–5 days, with a specific question to lower the reply barrier
  • After a meeting: Same day or next morning, while the conversation is fresh
  • After going silent: Immediately once you realize you dropped the ball

The problem: remembering to follow up

The challenge isn't writing the follow-up — it's remembering to send it. Without a system, follow-ups depend on memory, sticky notes, or scanning old email threads. Most slip through the cracks.
