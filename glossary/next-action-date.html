Next Action Date

A next action date is the specific date when you plan to take the next step on a deal — typically a follow-up email, call, or check-in. It's the single most important field for preventing deals from going stale.

Why next action dates matter

Ask any sales manager what separates reps who close from reps who don't, and the answer is almost always follow-up discipline. Next action dates are the mechanism for that discipline. Every open deal should have one. If a deal has no next action date, it's not being actively worked — it's just sitting there, aging.

The principle is simple: every deal either has a next action or it's dead. If you sent a proposal on Monday, the next action might be "follow up Thursday if no reply." When Thursday comes, you either get the reply or you follow up. Then you set the next date. The chain never breaks.

When to set the next action date

  • Immediately after every interaction. You just sent an email? Set the follow-up date before moving to the next task. This is the moment of highest context — don't rely on memory.
  • After every received reply. They responded? Great. Now when's the next thing you need to do? Set the date.
  • When creating a new deal. The moment a thread becomes a deal, it should get a next action date — even if that's just "check in next week."

Common mistakes

  • Setting it too far out. "I'll follow up in 2 weeks" usually means the deal dies quietly. For active deals, keep intervals to 2–5 days.
  • Setting it and forgetting. A next action date in a spreadsheet you don't check is worthless. The date needs to surface in your daily workflow.
  • Not setting one at all. The most common mistake. You send the email, move to the next task, and the deal has no forward motion scheduled.
