Sales Cycle Length

Sales cycle length is the average number of days between first contact with a prospect and closing the deal, measuring how long your typical sale takes from start to finish.

Typical benchmarks

  • Freelance / small services: 3–14 days. The prospect has an immediate need and a single decision-maker.
  • B2B services / consulting: 14–60 days. Multiple conversations, a proposal, and often internal approval.
  • Mid-market SaaS: 30–90 days. Demos, trials, procurement, and legal.
  • Enterprise: 90–180+ days. Committees, budgets, and compliance.

What lengthens the sales cycle

  • Slow follow-ups. Every day you wait to follow up adds at least a day to the cycle. Multiple forgotten follow-ups can add weeks.
  • Stale periods. Gaps where neither side contacts the other let momentum die. Restarting a cold conversation takes more effort than maintaining a warm one.
  • Poor timing. Sending at the wrong time means your email sits unread longer, delaying the next step.
  • Missing context. When you have to re-read the thread to remember where things stand, your reply is slower and less relevant.

How to shorten it

You can't control the prospect's internal process, but you can control the gaps between your touchpoints. Consistent, timely follow-ups — informed by open data and deal context — eliminate the dead time that inflates your sales cycle.
