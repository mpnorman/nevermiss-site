Follow-Up Reminder

A follow-up reminder is an automated alert that tells you when it's time to reach back out to a contact who hasn't responded, ensuring no conversation falls through the cracks.

Why reminders beat memory

When you're managing 10, 20, or 50 active conversations, memory isn't a system — it's a gamble. Follow-up reminders turn your intention ("I'll email Sarah on Thursday") into a guaranteed action by surfacing it at the right time.

Types of follow-up reminders

  • Calendar-based: You set a calendar event to remind yourself. Works, but clutters your calendar and provides no email context.
  • Snooze-based: Tools like Boomerang and Gmail's native snooze hide and resurface emails. Simple, but reactive — you only get the email back, not a prioritized view of all pending follow-ups.
  • Queue-based: Tools like NeverMiss build a daily follow-up queue. Every morning, you see a prioritized list of who to contact today — with open tracking, deal context, and one-click actions. Proactive, not reactive.

What makes a good reminder system

A notification alone isn't enough. A good follow-up reminder should include:

  • Context — deal stage, value, last message sent
  • Engagement signals — did they open your last email?
  • Action options — reply, snooze to a new date, or mark done
  • Prioritization — most urgent follow-ups first, not just chronological
